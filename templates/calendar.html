Math Encounters—What's Behind Door Number 2? — April 6

Starting fifty years ago, Monty Hall unwittingly turned the math world on its ear — students and professors alike — with a seemingly simple stunt on his game show, Let’s Make a Deal. In the midst of a trademark giveaway, he’d show contestants that one of the doors hiding prizes was a dud, and then give them the chance to switch from their current choice to the remaining unopened door. Since then, the debate has raged over whether it’s valuable to take Monty’s deal.

Join us for this one-of-a-kind lecture at MoMath—the only museum in North America dedicated to the topic of mathematics.
